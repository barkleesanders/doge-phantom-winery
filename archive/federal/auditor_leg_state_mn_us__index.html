Recently Released

Department of Natural Resources: Electronic Licensing System (April 8, 2026)

The Minnesota Department of Natural Resources (DNR) and Minnesota IT Services (MNIT) are modernizing the system used to sell hunting and fishing licenses and to title and register watercraft and recreational vehicles. The project is a year behind its original launch date and continues to encounter challenges. We identified ongoing concerns that could impact the project’s overall success and contribute to further delays in launching the new system.

Notices

  • The Office of the Legislative Auditor has over 50 years of strengthening accountability and providing legislative oversight of Minnesota state government.
    A brief overview of OLA’s history.

  • The Legislative Audit Commission Evaluation Subcommittee met on April 10, 2026, to narrow a list of proposed topics to 12 semifinalists for further consideration as OLA program evaluations in 2026–2027. These 12 topics are listed below:
    1. Adult Day Services (No. 69 from the Topic List)
    2. Attorney General’s Office (No. 95)
    3. Corporate Concentration in Minnesota (No. 96)
    4. DHS and County Collaboration on Service Approvals and Provision (No. 76)
    5. DHS System Modernization (No. 85)
    6. Minnesota Board of Behavioral Health and Therapy (No. 17)
    7. Minnesota Department of Health Mortuary Science Program (No. 67)
    8. Minnesota Paid Family Medical Leave (No. 29)
    9. Minnesota Research Tax Credit (No. 9)
    10. MPCA Feedlot Permitting (No. 52)
    11. Office of Cannabis Management (No. 101)
    12. Post-Secondary Enrollment Options (No. 43)
    OLA staff will prepare background information on these topics and meet with the Evaluation Subcommittee to further narrow this list in the coming weeks.
