## Narrative

To the Office of the California Attorney General,

I am writing as a California-resident citizen to bring to your attention a potential pattern of misrepresentation involving California-domiciled LLCs that may warrant review under Business and Professions Code §17200 (Unfair Competition Law) and the California Corporations Code provisions governing LLC registered offices.

**Subject entities (per California Secretary of State bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov):**

1. **eStCru LLC** — registered principal office: 422 Larkfield Center #400, Santa Rosa, CA 95403
2. **West Pike Inc.** — successor entity, same Santa Rosa address
3. **E Street Group LLC** — DC-domiciled but California-active

**The address itself.** 422 Larkfield Center #400 is a **Postal Plus virtual mailbox** — a private mail receiving service. Current published pricing is $10.99/month for the basic plan (verified via postscanmail.com / westpike.com cached pricing pages). It is not a winery, an office, or a place of business in any operational sense. Multiple unrelated LLCs use this same suite number as their "principal" office.

**Why this matters in California:**

Per the public 2024 House Clerk Financial Disclosure filed May 14, 2025 by Rep. Ilhan Omar (whose spouse Timothy Mynett is the principal of eStCru), the entity was reported jointly with Rose Lake Capital LLC at a combined value of up to **$30 million**. On April 23, 2026, in a recorded NY Post interview, Rep. Omar said the actual value is *"less than $100,000."* On April 20, 2026, an amended disclosure was filed showing $18K-$95K.

Whatever the true valuation, an entity reporting itself in federal sworn filings as a multi-million-dollar California winery — while operating only out of a $10.99/mo virtual mailbox — raises questions under:

- **CA Corp. Code §17708.06** (foreign LLC principal office requirements)
- **CA Bus. & Prof. Code §17200 et seq.** (unfair, fraudulent, or deceptive business acts)
- **CA Bus. & Prof. Code §17500** (false advertising — to the extent eStCru representations as a "California winery" reach California consumers)

I am not asking the AG to prejudge any of these questions. I am providing a public-record synthesis that the AG's Public Inquiry Unit may find useful for situational awareness, especially given that:
- House Oversight (Rep. Comer) has issued a subpoena-class document request on the same conduct
- House Ethics has received a referral
- House Select Committee on CCP is engaged on the foreign-state-ownership angle of the entity's principal investment (Ivanhoe Atlantic; parent 40% Chinese-state per Rep. Moolenaar Dec 9, 2025 letter)
