## 🎯 Headline finding

**HPX Nimba Holdings Inc. (BVI, Craigmuir Chambers, Road Town, Tortola, VG1110) holds 20,753,489 ordinary shares of Euronimba Limited as of the February 19, 2020 Annual Return — a 100% shareholding.**

This is a US-incorporated entity domiciled at a famous BVI offshore-services address. The HPX→Euronimba→SMFG chain is now confirmed end-to-end:

```
HPX Inc. (Delaware, CIK 0001662195)
    ↓ controls
HPX Nimba Holdings Inc. (BVI — Craigmuir Chambers, Road Town, Tortola)
    ↓ owns 100% of (per Euronimba 2020 AR)
Euronimba Limited (Jersey #49971)
    ↓ owns 85% of
Société des Mines de Fer de Guinée SA (SMFG, Guinea)
    ↓ holds the
Kon Kweni iron ore concession (Guinea)
```

Plus a sister chain: HPX Inc. → Euronimba Liberia Limited → rail evacuation rights to Port of Buchanan.

**No Mynett, Hailer, Hoffman, or Rose Lake-affiliated person appears anywhere in any of the 25 Euronimba records reviewed.** This is a definitive negative for the original "Rose Lake invested in Ivanhoe Atlantic" claim — the Jersey holdco's cap table is clean.

## HPX takeover (Sept 5, 2019 → Dec 2020)

**Sept 5, 2019** — HPX (then "High Power Exploration Inc.") announced acquisition of the 95% stake in SMFG previously held by BHP, Newmont Goldcorp and Orano (formerly Areva). Concession amended Dec 3, 2019 by Guinean National Assembly. Per [ivanhoeatlantic.com press release Feb 7, 2020](https://ivanhoeatlantic.com/relaunching-of-the-nimba-iron-ore-projects-environmental-and-social-impact-assessment-starting-with-public-consultations/).

**Dec 17, 2019 + Jan 27, 2020** — Euronimba issues 20,753,489 ordinary shares to HPX Nimba Holdings Inc. (BVI) — confirmed by the **2020-06-11 Special Resolution** which retroactively ratified the issuance.

**Feb 19, 2020 Annual Return** confirms HPX Nimba Holdings Inc. as sole/dominant shareholder with 20,753,489 ordinary shares.

**May 29, 2020, June 11, 2020, Dec 2, 2020** — three more Special Resolutions completing HPX's integration including new articles of association.

**May 19, 2022** — Authorised capital increased from $320M to $1B in preparation for further capital raises.

## Significance of HPX Nimba Holdings Inc. (BVI)

This is the **first time this entity name surfaces** in the Rose Lake / Ivanhoe Atlantic dossier. It does not appear in:
- HPX SEC Form D filings (the Delaware HPX Inc. is the issuer; HPX Nimba Holdings Inc. is a downstream sub)
- MIGA documentation (which names HPX directly as Guarantee Holder)
- UK Companies House (which names Euronimba Limited Jersey as ceased PSC of Ivanhoe Atlantic UK Ltd)
- Any prior EVIDENCE.md/NETWORK.md mention

**Implication for the reporter:** The HPX→Euronimba chain runs through a US-incorporated BVI-domiciled subsidiary. Anyone trying to trace HPX's Guinea/Liberia rail-deal cap table needs to look for "HPX Nimba Holdings Inc." in:
1. Delaware corporation records (if it's a Delaware C-corp like its parent)
2. BVI Financial Services Commission registry (Craigmuir Chambers is an offshore mailbox; the BVI registry would show directors + secretary if anything)
3. SEC filings — search EDGAR for "HPX Nimba Holdings" to see if it's separately filed

**The chain is offshore. BVI is a "qualifying jurisdiction" for FATCA but its beneficial-ownership disclosure regime is opaque.** The 2003 Euronimba structure ran through Jersey; HPX layered a BVI sub on top of the Jersey holdco.

## What this still doesn't answer

The OCR'd Annual Returns 2008-2018 contain a "FULL list of Shareholders is given overleaf" reference, but the second-page shareholder lists in some PDFs were not fully captured by tesseract OCR. **The 2018-02-16 and 2019-02-25 Annual Returns (the last pre-HPX cap table snapshots) should be re-OCR'd page-by-page or manually inspected to confirm there were no minor shareholders that may have rolled over after HPX's acquisition** — particularly any US-LLC names that could correspond to a Mynett-affiliated vehicle. As of this read, no such name surfaces.

The 15-Jan-2013 Register of Members was **removed from the public register by the JFSC** in March 2014 with the explanation "filed in error" — meaning that explicit shareholder snapshot is now redacted. This is unusual and could be revisited via a Mourant/JFSC FOI-type request if relevant.
